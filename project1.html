Snake Game
C#
OOP

Project Summary

Overview

This Snake game is inspired by the Bubu and Dudu theme and focuses on balancing love, risk, and strategy rather than just survival. The player controls a snake whose life is represented by a Love Meter, instead of traditional health.

Core Mechanics

The game revolves around a Love Meter system, where the snake’s survival depends on maintaining emotional health rather than avoiding instant death. Players can gain Super Power usages by collecting Bear icons and activate these abilities using specific keyboard keys, adding a layer of tactical decision-making. However, risk plays a major role—consuming Beer icons reduces the Love Meter and temporarily reverses all abilities for five seconds, turning helpful powers into disadvantages. Love can be restored by collecting Roses or purchasing refills from the in-game shop using earned currency, while a gambling mechanic allows players to risk currency for potential rewards at the cost of love if they lose. Player performance is tracked through a global leaderboard, showcasing overall scores and the Top 5 players.

Challenges

One of the main challenges in developing this game was managing the overall complexity of the system. The game includes multiple interconnected mechanics such as the Love Meter, power abilities, ability reversal, gambling, economy, and leaderboard. Ensuring that these systems worked together smoothly without introducing bugs required careful planning and iterative testing. Balancing the mechanics so that the gameplay felt engaging—without being overwhelming—was especially challenging, as each new feature had a direct impact on player experience.

Another significant challenge was game design and time management. With only one month of development time, decisions had to be made about which features were essential and which needed to be simplified or postponed. Designing a visually appealing interface while maintaining clear gameplay feedback required multiple design revisions. Managing development scope within a limited timeframe taught the importance of prioritization, trade-offs, and focusing on core gameplay quality rather than trying to implement every possible idea.
